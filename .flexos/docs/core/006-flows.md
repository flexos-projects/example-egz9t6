These user journeys map the critical paths visitors will take through the new website, focusing on how the site's architecture and content will guide them from discovery to conversion.

### Journey 1: The CEO (Referred Decision-Maker)

*   **Persona:** "Stefan," the non-technical CEO of a Bavarian SME.
*   **Goal:** Quickly validate if InnovationHub is a credible and suitable partner.
*   **Path:**
    1.  **Entry Point:** Direct visit to the **Homepage** (`/de/`) after a word-of-mouth referral.
    2.  **First Impression:** Reads the hero headline: "Ihr Partner für Digitale Transformation in Bayern." Feels understood. Scans the clean layout and professional design, feeling reassured.
    3.  **Exploration:** Scrolls down and sees the "Our Services" grid. Clicks on **"Digital Strategy Consulting"** (`/de/leistungen/digitalstrategie-beratung`).
    4.  **Validation:** On the service page, he reads the "Is This For You?" checklist and sees his exact business challenges listed. He is impressed by the clear, benefit-focused language.
    5.  **Proof:** Clicks on the linked **Case Study** for a manufacturing client. He doesn't read every word but scans the "Challenge" and "Impact" sections, noting the impressive metrics and the familiar client logo. Trust is established.
    6.  **Action:** Clicks the prominent "Schedule a Consultation" CTA at the end of the case study. He is taken to the **Contact Page** (`/de/kontakt`).
    7.  **Conversion:** Fills out the form with his details, feeling confident he is reaching out to the right experts.

### Journey 2: The Project Manager (SEO-Driven Researcher)

*   **Persona:** "Martina," the tech-savvy Head of Operations.
*   **Goal:** Find an expert partner and build a business case.
*   **Path:**
    1.  **Entry Point:** Searches Google for `agile webanwendung entwicklung münchen`. Lands directly on an **"Insights" Article** (`/de/einblicke/agile-methoden-fuer-den-mittelstand`) written by Lena Meyer.
    2.  **Authority Building:** Reads the article and is impressed by the practical, expert advice. Notes the author's name and title.
    3.  **Deeper Dive:** Clicks on "Our Work" (`/de/projekte`) in the main navigation. Filters by "Custom Web Applications."
    4.  **Technical Vetting:** Clicks into a detailed **Case Study**. She reads the "Solution" section carefully, assessing their technical approach and the technologies used.
    5.  **Team Vetting:** Navigates to the **"About Us" Page** (`/de/ueber-uns`). Reads the professional bios of Dr. Müller (impressed by the PhD) and Markus Schmidt (notes his cloud architecture expertise). The team's credentials solidify her confidence.
    6.  **Action:** Copies the link to the "About Us" page and the relevant Case Study into an email to her CEO, Stefan, with the note: "I think I found our partner." -> **Consideration & Internal Championing.**

### Journey 3: The Mobile User (Quick Contact)

*   **Persona:** Anyone who has heard the company name and needs to get in touch quickly.
*   **Goal:** Find a phone number or address while on the go.
*   **Path:**
    1.  **Entry Point:** Searches "InnovationHub Solutions GmbH" on their mobile phone. Clicks the top organic result, landing on the mobile **Homepage**.
    2.  **Immediate Access:** The phone number in the site header is immediately visible and tappable (`tel:` link). The "Contact" button is also prominent in the mobile menu.
    3.  **Exploration:** Clicks the hamburger menu to see the site structure. Taps on **"Contact"**.
    4.  **Conversion:** On the contact page, the phone number and address are at the top. The user taps the phone number to initiate a call directly from their browser. Goal achieved in under 15 seconds.
